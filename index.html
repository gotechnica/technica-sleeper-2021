2021 Organizer Application FAQ

What is a "hackathon"?

+

A hackathon is a technology-focused event where individuals and teams work on new projects in a short period of time. You can build anything you want. An app, a robot, a website. The only limit is your imagination! Technica is the world's largest hackathon for all-women and non-binary people.

Why make an all-women and non-binary hackathon?

+

Women, non-binary, and gender non-conforming people are underrepresented in the tech world, and it’s up to everyone to change that! Technica’s goal is to create a welcoming environment where people from underrepresented genders can build cool projects and share their stories!

For the past 6 years, we have gathered thousands of individuals from underrepresented backgrounds in tech for a weekend of learning, building, and connection. We believe that the best way to bridge the gender divide in tech is by getting the next generation of female and nonbinary engineers excited about building and creating.

Will Technica 2021 be in-person, online, or hybrid?

+

Although we're waiting to see the efficacy of the vaccine rollout before making a final decision, our current plan is to host an unprecedented hybrid model of a hackathon in 2021. This involves both in-person (regional meetups, socializing, food, etc.) and virtual components (workshops, tech talks). Organizer bonding and organizer meetings will very likely be in-person in the fall and potentially summer.

Who is eligible to apply?

+

University of Maryland, College Park students of all majors and backgrounds are encouraged to apply! You don't need to be a CS major or have a technical background to help; we have teams whose work involves fields like design, event planning, psychology, accounting, and others.

What is the application process like?

+

The application is open now until April 5 at 11:59PM EST. After the directing team reviews the applications, interviews for specific teams will take place until April 15. You will receive your decision on April 16!
